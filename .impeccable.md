## Design Context

### Users
Internal analysts and operators working on Sintra address selection and parcel review. They use the tool in a practical, work-focused setting to inspect source layers, compare geometry, and tune ranking logic.

### Brand Personality
Calm, precise, dependable. The interface should feel like a trusted field tool rather than a demo or dashboard theater. It should reduce anxiety, make state obvious, and support careful decisions.

### Aesthetic Direction
Quiet utilitarian map tooling with warm neutral surfaces, restrained accent colors, and clear hierarchy. The explorer should feel related to the review app: dense but readable, tactile without being flashy, and focused on data inspection rather than decoration.

### Design Principles
- Keep the main action surfaces unambiguous and easy to scan.
- Use restrained color to distinguish layers, not to decorate the page.
- Prefer explicit toggles, readable labels, and obvious selection state.
- Make the map secondary to the layer controls when necessary, but never bury the map.
- Keep the interface lightweight and fast enough to explore a full source cache without friction.
